Eastern Dragon

Website Redesign

In the Eastern Dragon Project, my goal was to recreate the Eastern Dragon’s website and logo. The Eastern Dragon’s original website lacked some design qualities that I was looking for. It was rather plain looking, and clearly hadn’t been updated in some time. There was nothing responsive about the site, and it didn’t include the menu in the actual site. I wanted to change all of this, to offer the best experience possible to mobile and desktop users. The logo needed to a simple, yet intuitive logo design. I chose a piece of sushi with some chopsticks. This brings the thought of Asian cuisine and sushi into one logo.

The website has a modern and sophisticated theme to it. I wanted the user to feel invited, but not at home. This should give the viewer the feeling they are about to go out to eat, and want something high class. I put lots of emphasis on food throughout the page, to attempt let the user feel hungry for what this restaurant is offering. The font family I chose (Open Sans) is sharp and professional. The color scheme is mostly black and white, with a few colors mixed in. This puts a strong emphasis on the colorful images. The project uses a transparent overlay on top of the header image, to bring contrast to the white text. This allows for legibility.

I created this project using my favorite text editor, Atom, with a few plugins for Sass and FTP abilities. I coded everything to be perfectly responsive. I used to flexbox model to allow for responsive content without the need to add media queries for each element. On the front page, I took one of the images from the original website and used it for my own. I then added an “About” header with some Lorem Ipsum as filler text. For the menu section, I took the PDFs from the site, and converted them to SVGs. I then converted these SVGs into JPEG thumbnails. When clicking on the thumbnails, you are brought to the raw SVG, where you can easily read everything on the menu. I even took the liberty of editing parts of the menu to represent changes they had made to it.

When creating this project, I learned how important colors really are. I wasn’t focusing on colors much in the beginning, but throughout the project I realized I should keep the white and black shades as main components, and only use a small amount of color.
